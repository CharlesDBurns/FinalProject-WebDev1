Parks

  • Forger Park

    Location: Fallas, GA.

    Description:

    Spacious yet filled with shady trees, Forger Park is one of the most underrated parks in all of Georgia. It's home to the longest stretches of flatlands in Georgia, with just enough trees to provide a comfortable temperature that's just perfect for camping most of the year. A great place for beginners to camp out and see how they like it and if they want to up the experience farther from civilization.

  • Templar National Park

    Location: London, NV

    Description:

    Nationally known for it's park center with an abundance of Templar artifacts from the Medieval Ages. Located on one of Nevada's many mountain ranges, it features breath-taking views that make hikes all the more worth it. It is not recommended for beginners to start off with the hikes at this park, however, it has been done. Besides the the Templar museum in the park center there are several different hikes available with and without guides as well as available camping spots for those who enjoy the wilderness.

  • Knotmadup Park

    Location: Reel, MN.

    Description:

    As well as being in Reel Minnesota, it has thick forests and wide paths for all those forest-lovers out there. This park is great for seasoned campers who want a real outdoors experience as this park has only one building and few main paths making it ideal for camping out. Definitely not for beginners. If you're planning on camping here you should definitely bring bug spray.
